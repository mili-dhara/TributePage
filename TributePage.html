Pichai Sundararajan

A key player in the technological revolution of the 21st century

Here's a timeline of Sundar Pichai's life:

  • 1972 - Born in Tamil Nadu, India
  • 1993 - Pichai graduates with a Bachelor of Technology degree from the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur.
  • 1995 - Earns a Master of Science degree from Stanford University in material sciences and engineering.
  • 2002 - Joins Google as a product manager, overseeing the development of the Google Toolbar.
  • 2004 - Becomes the head of product and design for Google's web browser, Chrome.
  • 2008 - Leads the development of Chrome OS, an operating system designed for web applications.
  • 2011 - Takes on responsibility for Google's suite of apps, including Gmail, Drive, and Docs.
  • 2013 - Becomes the senior vice president of Android, the world's most popular mobile operating system.
  • 2014 - Pichai is promoted to oversee all of Google's products, including search, ads, maps, and more.
  • 2015 - Google reorganizes under a new parent company called Alphabet, with Pichai becoming CEO of Google.
  • 2016 - Introduces Google Assistant, the company's artificial intelligence-powered virtual assistant.
  • 2017 - Testifies before Congress on issues related to user privacy and Google's data collection practices.
  • 2018 - Google announces plans to build a new campus in San Jose, California, under Pichai's leadership.
  • 2019 - Pichai becomes CEO of Alphabet, in addition to his role as CEO of Google.
  • 2020 - Leads Google's response to the COVID-19 pandemic, including the launch of various tools to help people work and learn remotely.
  • 2021 - Google announces plans to invest $7 billion in expanding its presence in the United States, with Pichai citing a desire to create economic opportunities across the country.

"Sundar has a tremendous ability to see what's ahead and mobilize teams around the super important stuff."

--Founder and Ex-CEO of Google, Larry Page
